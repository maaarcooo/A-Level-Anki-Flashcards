What is work done by a force equivalent to? | A transfer of energy
What is the unit of work done? | Newton metres (N m) or Joules (J)
What is the relationship between 1 N m and 1 J? | 1 N m = 1 J
What does the work done by a resultant force on a system equal? | The change in energy in that system
Define mechanical work. | The amount of energy transferred when an external force causes an object to move over a certain distance
What is the equation for work done when force is parallel to displacement? | W = Fs
In W = Fs, what does W represent? | Work done (J)
In W = Fs, what does F represent? | Average force applied (N)
In W = Fs, what does s represent? | Displacement (m)
When pushing a block, what is work done against? | Friction
Where does dissipated energy go when pushing a block? | To the surroundings by heating and as sound
What is the equation for work done when force is at an angle to displacement? | W = Fs cos θ
In W = Fs cos θ, what does θ represent? | The angle between the direction of force and the motion of the object
When θ = 0 in W = Fs cos θ, what does cos θ equal? | 1, so the equation becomes W = Fs
For horizontal motion with angled force, which trigonometric function is used? | cos θ
For vertical motion with angled force, which trigonometric function is used? | sin θ
Which component of force is used when calculating work done? | The component parallel to the displacement
Define power. | The rate of doing work or the rate of energy transfer
What is the equation for power in terms of work done? | P = ΔW/Δt
In P = ΔW/Δt, what does P represent? | Power (W)
In P = ΔW/Δt, what does ΔW represent? | Change in work done (J)
In P = ΔW/Δt, what does Δt represent? | Change in time (s)
How is power increased? (2 ways) | Greater energy transfer (work done) OR energy transferred over a shorter period of time
What is the equation for power when an object moves at constant velocity with constant force? | P = Fv
In P = Fv, what does F represent? | Force (N)
In P = Fv, what does v represent? | Velocity (m s⁻¹)
What condition must the force meet when using P = Fv? | The force must be in the direction of the velocity
If force is not in the direction of velocity when using P = Fv, what must be used instead? | The component of force in the direction of velocity
How can work done be found from a force-displacement graph? | By calculating the area under the graph
Why does area under a force-displacement graph equal work done? | Because Work done = Force × Displacement
The same work done results from what two scenarios? | A small force over a long displacement OR a large force over a small displacement
What is a variable force? | A force on an object that is not constant
Can W = Fs and P = Fv be used for variable forces? | No, work done must be found from the area under the force-displacement graph
If a varying force increases, what happens to an object's acceleration? | It increases (and vice versa)
Define efficiency. | The ratio of the useful power output from a system to its total power input
What does high efficiency mean for a system? | Most of the energy transferred is useful
What does low efficiency mean for a system? | Most of the energy transferred is wasted
What is the equation for efficiency? | Efficiency = (Useful power output / Total power input) × 100%
How is power defined in terms of energy? | Energy transferred per unit of time (Power = Energy/Time)
What is the efficiency equation in terms of energy? | Efficiency = (Useful energy output / Total energy input) × 100%
In a lightbulb, which energy is useful and which is wasted? | Light energy is useful; heat energy is wasted
In a heater, which energy is useful and which is wasted? | Thermal (heat) energy is useful; sound energy is wasted
Can efficiency be greater than 100%? | No, efficiency is between 0% and 100% (or 0 and 1 as a ratio)
Does efficiency have units? | No, efficiency has no units as it is a ratio
State the Principle of Conservation of Energy. | Energy cannot be created or destroyed, it can only be transferred from one form to another
What remains constant in a closed system according to conservation of energy? | The total amount of energy
What is dissipated energy? | Energy that is transferred to the surroundings, usually regarded as wasted energy
What forms does dissipated/wasted energy commonly take? | Thermal (heat), light, or sound energy
Is any energy transfer 100% efficient? | No
What energy transfer occurs in a falling object (in a vacuum)? | Gravitational potential energy → kinetic energy
What energy transfer occurs in a battery? | Chemical energy → electrical energy
What energy transfer occurs in a horizontal mass on a spring? | Elastic potential energy → kinetic energy
When an object travels up a rough inclined surface, what is the energy equation? | Loss in kinetic energy = Gain in gravitational potential energy + Work done against friction
What is the equation for kinetic energy? | Eₖ = ½mv²
What is the equation for gravitational potential energy? | ΔEₚ = mgΔh
What is the equation for elastic potential energy? | Eₑ = ½k(ΔL)²
What is kinetic energy? | The energy an object has due to its motion (or velocity)
What happens to kinetic energy as an object moves faster? | It increases
When an object is falling, what happens to its kinetic energy? | It gains kinetic energy as it gains speed
Where does a falling object's kinetic energy come from? | From the gravitational potential energy it is losing
In Eₖ = ½mv², what does Eₖ represent? | Kinetic energy (J)
In Eₖ = ½mv², what does m represent? | Mass (kg)
In Eₖ = ½mv², what does v represent? | Velocity (m s⁻¹)
In the kinetic energy equation, which quantity is squared? | Only the speed (v), not the mass or the ½
What is gravitational potential energy? | Energy stored in a mass due to its position in a gravitational field
What happens to gravitational potential energy when a mass is lifted up? | It gains gravitational potential energy
What happens to gravitational potential energy when a mass falls? | It loses gravitational potential energy
In ΔEₚ = mgΔh, what does ΔEₚ represent? | Gravitational potential energy (J)
In ΔEₚ = mgΔh, what does m represent? | Mass (kg)
In ΔEₚ = mgΔh, what does g represent? | Gravitational field strength (9.81 N kg⁻¹)
In ΔEₚ = mgΔh, what does Δh represent? | Change in height (m)
What is the gravitational potential energy usually taken as at ground level? | Zero
When is the equation ΔEₚ = mgΔh valid? | For energy changes in a uniform gravitational field (such as near Earth's surface)
What type of relationship exists between gravitational potential energy and height? | A linear relationship
What type of energy does a kinetic energy describe? | Energy of a moving object
What type of energy does gravitational potential energy describe? | Energy stored due to position in a gravitational field
What is the correct symbol for gravitational potential energy in equations? | ΔEₚ
What common exam mistake involves work done calculations? | Choosing the incorrect force which is not parallel to the direction of movement
When calculating work done with an angled force, what must you do first? | Resolve the force vector to find the component parallel to displacement
When calculating areas from force-displacement graphs, what must you check? | The units on the axes (convert kN to N, km to m for SI units)
How can efficiency be expressed? | As a ratio (between 0 and 1) or as a percentage (between 0% and 100%)
How do you convert efficiency ratio to percentage? | Multiply by 100
On a trampoline, what energy conversion occurs when bouncing upward? | Elastic potential energy → kinetic energy
On a trampoline, what energy conversion occurs when rising in the air? | Kinetic energy → gravitational potential energy
