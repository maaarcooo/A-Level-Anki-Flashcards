What is a projectile? | A particle moving freely (non-powered), under gravity, in a two-dimensional plane
What is time of flight in projectile motion? | How long the projectile is in the air
For typical projectile motion, how does time to maximum height relate to total time? | Time to maximum height is half of the total time
What is maximum height in projectile motion? | The height at which the projectile is momentarily at rest
What is the vertical velocity at maximum height in projectile motion? | Zero
What is range in projectile motion? | The horizontal distance travelled by the projectile
Are the horizontal and vertical components of projectile motion dependent or independent? | Independent of each other
What is the horizontal acceleration in projectile motion (ignoring air resistance)? | Zero (velocity remains constant)

How do you find the vertical component of initial velocity in projectile motion? | u sin θ (where u is resultant velocity, θ is angle to horizontal)
How do you find the horizontal component of initial velocity in projectile motion? | u cos θ (where u is resultant velocity, θ is angle to horizontal)
What happens to horizontal velocity during projectile motion (ignoring air resistance)? | It remains constant
What happens to vertical velocity during projectile motion? | It changes due to gravitational acceleration

What are drag forces? | Forces that oppose the motion of an object moving through a fluid (gas or liquid)
Give two examples of drag forces. | Friction and air resistance
In which direction do drag forces act? | In the opposite direction to the motion of the object
Can drag forces speed up an object? | No, they can only slow down or maintain constant speed
What energy conversion occurs due to drag forces? | Kinetic energy is converted into heat and sound
What is lift? | An upwards force on an object moving through a fluid, perpendicular to the fluid flow
What happens to drag force as speed increases? | It increases
What factors affect air resistance on a vehicle? | Cross-sectional area, shape, altitude, temperature, humidity
Why do aeroplanes travel at high altitudes? | There is less air resistance because the air is less dense
What is terminal velocity? | The maximum velocity an object can reach when drag force equals gravitational pull
What happens to acceleration when terminal velocity is reached? | Acceleration becomes zero
For a falling object, when is terminal velocity reached? | When the drag force equals the weight of the object
How does mass affect terminal velocity? | A greater mass means a higher terminal velocity
If skydiver A is heavier than skydiver B, who has the higher terminal velocity? | Skydiver A (the heavier one)
What happens to velocity when a skydiver deploys their parachute? | Velocity decreases (decelerates) to a lower terminal velocity
What is the aim of the required practical for determination of g? | To calculate the value of acceleration due to gravity by measuring time for a ball-bearing to fall a certain distance
What is the independent variable in the determination of g experiment? | Height, h
What is the dependent variable in the determination of g experiment? | Time, t
What equipment is used to release the ball-bearing consistently in the g experiment? | An electromagnet
What equipment measures the time in the determination of g experiment? | Light gates connected to a timer
What equation is used to analyse results in the determination of g experiment? | s = ut + ½at², rearranged to 2h/t = gt + 2u
What should be plotted on the axes to find g graphically? | 2h/t on y-axis against t on x-axis
What does the gradient of a 2h/t against t graph represent? | Acceleration due to gravity, g
What does the y-intercept of a 2h/t against t graph represent? | 2u (twice the initial velocity)
What is a systematic error in the determination of g experiment? | Residual magnetism after electromagnet is switched off causes t to be recorded as longer than it should be
Name two random errors in the determination of g experiment. | Large uncertainty in h from metre rule precision, parallax error from reading h, ball not falling accurately through centre of light gates
How are random errors reduced in the determination of g experiment? | By repeating the experiment for each value of h at least 3-5 times and finding an average time
What safety precaution relates to the electromagnet? | Only switch on the current once everything is set up, keep water away to reduce electrocution risk
Why is a cushion placed below the glass tube? | To catch the ball-bearing so it doesn't roll off or damage the surface

How do you calculate the error in t? | Half the range of each set of time values
How do you find the percentage uncertainty in 2h/t? | Add the percentage uncertainties of h and t
What is the line of worst fit? | The steepest line that still passes through all error bars
