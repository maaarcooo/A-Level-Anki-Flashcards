What does the Young modulus measure? | The ability of a material to withstand changes in length with an added load
What information does the Young modulus give about a material? | Information about the stiffness of a material
Why is the Young modulus useful for engineers? | To make sure the materials they are using can withstand sufficient forces
How is the Young modulus defined? | The ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain
What is the equation for Young modulus? | Young Modulus = Tensile stress / Tensile strain = FL / (AΔL)
What does F represent in the Young modulus equation? | Force (N)
What does L represent in the Young modulus equation? | Original length (m)
What does A represent in the Young modulus equation? | Cross-sectional area (m²)
What does ΔL represent in the Young modulus equation? | Extension (m)
Why are the units of Young modulus pascals (Pa)? | Because strain is dimensionless, so Young modulus has the same units as stress (Pa)
What is the typical order of magnitude for Young modulus values? | Gigapascals (GPa)
What is the Young modulus of aluminium? | 70 GPa
What is the Young modulus of copper? | 117 GPa
What is the Young modulus of brass? | 120 GPa
What is the Young modulus of iron? | 170 GPa
What is the Young modulus of nickel? | 210 GPa
What is the Young modulus of steel? | 110 GPa
How can the Young modulus be determined from a stress-strain graph? | It equals the gradient of the graph when it is linear (straight line)
In which region of a stress-strain graph does the gradient equal the Young modulus? | The region where Hooke's Law is obeyed (linear region)
What does the area under the linear region of a stress-strain graph represent? | The energy stored per unit volume of the material
A material has a Young modulus of 70 GPa. What material could this be? | Aluminium
A material has a Young modulus of 210 GPa. What material could this be? | Nickel
Mnemonic: How to remember whether stress or strain comes first in the Young modulus equation? | "When you're stressed, you show the strain" - Stress ÷ Strain
What is the aim of the Young modulus required practical? | To measure the Young modulus of a metal in the form of a wire
What is the independent variable in the Young modulus experiment? | Force (or load) in newtons
What is the dependent variable in the Young modulus experiment? | Extension in metres
What are the control variables in the Young modulus experiment? | Original length of wire, thickness of wire, and the metal used
What is the purpose of the G-clamp in the Young modulus experiment? | To stabilise the apparatus
What is the purpose of the pulley in the Young modulus experiment? | To allow the mass to hang vertically and introduce less friction than the edge of the table
What is the purpose of the tape marker in the Young modulus experiment? | To accurately measure the extension with the applied load (it should touch the ruler)
What is the purpose of the wooden blocks in the Young modulus experiment? | To clamp the wire together to keep it taut and straight
What is used to measure the diameter of the wire? | A micrometer screw gauge or digital callipers
What is the resolution of a metre ruler? | 1 mm
What is the resolution of a micrometer? | 0.001 mm
How many diameter readings should be taken and why? | At least 3 readings to find an average
What mass increments are typically added in the Young modulus experiment? | 100 g masses
How many mass additions should be made in the experiment? | At least 5 to 10 times
How is the load (force) calculated from the mass? | By multiplying each mass by g (9.81 N kg⁻¹)
What is the formula for the cross-sectional area of the wire? | A = πd²/4, where d is the diameter
What graph should be plotted to find the Young modulus experimentally? | Load (force) against extension
What does the gradient of a force-extension graph represent? | F/ΔL
How is the Young modulus calculated from the gradient of a force-extension graph? | Young modulus = gradient × (L/A), where L is original length and A is cross-sectional area
What equation relates force to extension using Young modulus? | F = (Young Modulus × A / L) × ΔL
When comparing F = (Young Modulus × A / L) × ΔL to y = mx, what is y? | F (force)
When comparing F = (Young Modulus × A / L) × ΔL to y = mx, what is x? | ΔL (extension)
When comparing F = (Young Modulus × A / L) × ΔL to y = mx, what is the gradient m? | (Young Modulus × A) / L
What systematic error can be reduced by using a vernier scale? | Imprecise extension readings
What happens if the wire is extended past its elastic limit? | It will be permanently deformed
How can you check the wire hasn't exceeded its elastic limit? | Remove the load and check the wire returns to its original length before taking new readings
What is a source of random error when reading the extension? | Parallax error from reading the marker on the ruler
How can random errors be reduced in the Young modulus experiment? | By repeating the experiment for all loads and finding an average extension
How can uncertainty in cross-sectional area be reduced? | By measuring the diameter in several places and calculating an average
Why should safety goggles be worn during the experiment? | In case the wire snaps
What safety precaution should be taken regarding the mass hanger? | Keep a cushion or soft surface directly below it in case it falls off
How can the experiment be improved to get more accurate results? | Repeat readings and use a longer length of wire
What is stress? | Force per unit area (σ = F/A)
What is strain? | Extension divided by original length (ε = ΔL/L)
What are the units of stress? | Pascals (Pa) or N m⁻²
What are the units of strain? | Dimensionless (no units)
What does a steeper gradient on a stress-strain graph indicate? | A higher Young modulus (stiffer material)
In the linear region of a stress-strain graph, what law is being obeyed? | Hooke's Law
What conversion is needed for mm² to m²? | 1 mm² = 1 × 10⁻⁶ m²
How do you convert a percentage strain to a decimal? | Divide by 100 (e.g., 0.50% = 0.005)
If a wire increases in length by 0.50%, what is the strain? | 0.005
Why is a long wire preferred in the Young modulus experiment? | It produces a larger, more measurable extension for the same stress
