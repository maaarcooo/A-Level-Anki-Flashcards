What is diffraction? | The spreading out of waves after they pass through a narrow gap or around an obstruction
What phenomenon describes waves spreading out after passing through a narrow gap or around an obstruction? | Diffraction
When are diffraction effects most noticeable? | When the wavelength of the wave and the width of the gap are similar in size
For light, what type of slits are required for significant diffraction effects? | Very narrow slits (similar in size to the wavelength of light)
What does the diffraction pattern of light passing through a single slit consist of? | A series of light and dark fringes on a screen
What causes bright fringes in a single slit diffraction pattern? | Constructive interference of each part of the wavefront as it passes through the slit
What causes dark fringes in a single slit diffraction pattern? | Destructive interference of each part of the wavefront as it passes through the slit
Bright fringes are areas of what type of intensity? | Maximum intensity
Dark fringes are areas of what type of intensity? | Zero or minimum intensity
How does the central maximum in single slit diffraction compare to other bright fringes? | It is much wider and brighter than the other bright fringes
How does the central maximum in single slit diffraction compare to double-slit diffraction? | It is much wider than that of the double-slit diffraction pattern
What happens to the brightness of maxima as the order increases in single slit diffraction? | They get dimmer (narrower and less bright)
What is the central bright fringe in single slit diffraction called? | The central maximum
What intensity do dark fringes have in single slit diffraction? | Zero intensity
What happens to the intensity of bright fringes moving away from the central maximum? | The intensity gets less
Why is the central maximum bright white when white light passes through a single slit? | Because constructive interference from all colours happens at this point
What do non-central maxima consist of when white light passes through a single slit? | A spectrum
Which colour appears nearest to the central maximum when white light is diffracted? | Violet/blue (shortest wavelength)
Why does violet/blue light appear nearest to the central maximum? | Because it is diffracted less (shorter wavelength)
Which colour appears furthest from the central maximum when white light is diffracted? | Red (longest wavelength)
Why does red light appear furthest from the central maximum? | Because it is diffracted more (longer wavelength)
What happens to the colours further away from the central maximum in white light diffraction? | They look blurry and the spectra eventually merge without space between them
As maxima move further from the central maximum in white light diffraction, how do the wavelengths change? | Blue wavelengths decrease and red wavelengths increase
How does the central maxima intensity of white light compare to monochromatic light in single slit diffraction? | Equal in intensity
What happens to non-central maxima when white light passes through a single slit compared to monochromatic light? | They are wider and less intense
What happens to fringe spacing between maxima further from the central maximum in white light diffraction? | The fringe spacing gets smaller
In Young's double slit experiment, what two phenomena occur? | Light passing through the two slits interferes, and each slit also diffracts light like a single slit
What is the pattern of the double-slit interference pattern? | Equally spaced intensity peaks with maxima of equal intensity
What is distinctive about the single-slit intensity pattern? | A distinctive central maximum and subsequent maxima at lower intensity
What is the combined intensity pattern of single and double slit called? | Equally spaced bright fringes within a single slit 'envelope'
What happens to waves when the gap size increases compared to the wavelength? | The waves spread out less after passing through the gap
What happens to diffraction when the wavelength passing through a gap is increased? | The wave diffracts more
What happens to the angle of diffraction when wavelength increases? | The angle of diffraction increases
What happens to the width of bright maxima when wavelength increases? | The width increases
What type of diffraction pattern does red light produce compared to blue light? | Wide fringes (red has longer wavelength)
What type of diffraction pattern does blue light produce compared to red light? | Narrow fringes (blue has shorter wavelength)
What happens to the angle of diffraction when the slit is made narrower? | The angle of diffraction is greater
What happens to waves when the slit is made narrower? | The waves spread out more beyond the slit
What happens to the intensity of maxima when the slit is made narrower? | The intensity decreases
What happens to the width of the central maxima when the slit is made narrower? | The width increases
What happens to fringe spacing when the slit is made narrower? | The spacing between fringes is wider
What happens to fringes when slits are made wider? | The fringes become narrower
What property of a wave remains constant during diffraction? | Wavelength (distance between wavefronts)
What property of a wave changes during diffraction? | Amplitude
What is a diffraction grating? | A piece of optical equipment consisting of a large number of very thin, equally spaced parallel slits carved into a glass plate
What does a diffraction grating do to monochromatic light? | Diffracts it into bright and dark fringes
What does a diffraction grating do to white light? | Diffracts it into its different wavelength components
Why are diffraction gratings useful compared to double slits? | They create a sharper pattern with narrower, brighter bright fringes and wider, darker dark regions
What is the diffraction grating equation? | d sin θ = nλ
In the diffraction grating equation, what does n represent? | The order of the maxima (number of maxima away from central, n = 0)
In the diffraction grating equation, what does d represent? | The distance between the slits on the grating (in metres)
In the diffraction grating equation, what does θ represent? | The angle of diffraction of light of order n from the normal (in degrees)
In the diffraction grating equation, what does λ represent? | The wavelength of light from the source (in metres)
What determines the size of a diffraction grating? | The number of lines per millimetre (lines/mm) or lines per metre
What symbol represents the number of lines per unit length on a diffraction grating? | N
What is the equation relating slit spacing d to the number of lines N? | d = 1/N
How is the angular separation of each maxima calculated? | By rearranging the grating equation to make θ the subject
What happens to angular separation as the order of maxima increases? | Angular separation increases
How do you find the angular separation between two maxima? | Subtract the smaller angle from the larger one
What is the maximum angle of diffraction at which maxima can be seen? | 90° (beam at right angles to the diffraction grating)
What is sin θ equal to at maximum angle of diffraction? | 1
What is the equation for the highest order of maxima visible? | n = d/λ
If the calculated value of n is a decimal, how do you determine the highest visible order? | Round down to the nearest integer
What is the path difference at the zeroth-order maximum? | Zero
What is the path difference at the first-order maxima? | λ (one wavelength)
What is the path difference at the nth order maxima? | nλ
What is the equation for sin θ at the first-order maxima? | sin θ = λ/d
What is the equation for sin θ at the second-order maxima? | sin θ = 2λ/d
What is the general equation for sin θ at the nth order maxima? | sin θ = nλ/d
What are diffraction gratings useful for? | Separating light of different wavelengths with high resolution
Name one use of diffraction gratings in spectrometers. | Analyse light from stars / analyse composition of stars / chemical analysis / measure red shift / measure wavelength of light from stars
How are diffraction gratings used in X-ray crystallography? | X-rays directed at thin crystal sheets form diffraction patterns used to measure atomic spacing
Why do thin crystal sheets work as diffraction gratings for X-rays? | The wavelength of X-rays is similar in size to the gaps between atoms
What is a monochromator used for? | To specifically analyse a wavelength emitted by molecules or excite molecules with a specific wavelength
What are diffraction gratings used for in optical fibres? | To select the optimum wavelength of light for use in an optical fibre
What is the approximate wavelength range of X-rays? | 1 × 10⁻⁸ to 4 × 10⁻¹³ m
What is the approximate wavelength range of UV radiation? | 4 × 10⁻⁷ to 1 × 10⁻⁸ m
What is the approximate wavelength range of sound waves? | 1.72 × 10⁻² to 17 m
What is the approximate wavelength range of radio waves? | 0.1 to 10⁶ m
For diffraction to occur with X-rays, what size gap is needed? | Gaps between atoms in crystalline solids (similar to X-ray wavelength)
What type of interference produces bright fringes? | Constructive interference
What type of interference produces dark fringes? | Destructive interference
In the context of diffraction gratings, what does 'high resolution' mean? | Sharp, distinct separation of different wavelengths
Where is angle θ measured from in the diffraction grating equation? | From the normal (centre line)
