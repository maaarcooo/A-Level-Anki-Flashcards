What does the Data Protection Act 1998 protect? | Personal data from being misused
What is personal data? | Any data which can be used to identify a living person
What is a data subject? | An individual who can be identified by personal data
Give three examples of personal data | Name, address, date of birth (also: race, religion)
Does data that can identify a person only when combined with other data count as personal data? | Yes, it is also classified as personal data
What two types of storage does the Data Protection Act apply to? | Computers and organised paper filing systems
How many data protection principles are there under the DPA 1998? | Eight
State Data Protection Principle 1 | Personal data shall be processed fairly and lawfully
State Data Protection Principle 2 | Personal data shall be obtained only for one or more specified and lawful purposes
State Data Protection Principle 3 | Personal data shall be adequate, relevant and not excessive for its purpose(s)
State Data Protection Principle 4 | Personal data shall be accurate and where necessary kept up to date
State Data Protection Principle 5 | Personal data shall not be kept for longer than is necessary for its purpose(s)
State Data Protection Principle 6 | Personal data shall be processed in accordance with the rights of data subjects under this Act
State Data Protection Principle 7 | Appropriate technical and organisational measures shall be taken against unauthorised or unlawful processing of personal data and against accidental loss or destruction of, or damage to, personal data
State Data Protection Principle 8 | Personal data shall not be transferred to a country or territory outside the European Economic Area, unless that country or territory ensures an adequate level of data protection
Under the DPA, what right does a data subject have regarding viewing their data? | The right to request a copy of the data held about them
Under the DPA, what right does a data subject have regarding incorrect data? | The right to correct the data held about them (have inaccurate data rectified)
Under the DPA, what right does a data subject have regarding marketing? | The right to prevent marketing using contact details provided by the data subject
What is the right to be forgotten? | The right to have personal data deleted in certain circumstances
What is data portability? | The right allowing data subjects to obtain and reuse their personal data for their own purposes across different services
What legislation replaced the Data Protection Act 1998 in 2018? | The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018
What is a Subject Access Request (SAR)? | A request from a data subject for a company to provide a copy of all personal data held about them
Who is a Data Controller? | A member of staff appointed by a company to ensure the Data Protection Act principles are not breached and to communicate with the Information Commissioner
Who can data subjects complain to about data protection breaches? | The Information Commissioner
Name three exemptions from the Data Protection Act | Domestic purposes, law enforcement, intelligence services processing
What is the domestic purposes exemption from the DPA? | If you only use personal data for writing to friends/family or taking pictures for your own enjoyment, you are not subject to the DPA
What is the law enforcement exemption from the DPA? | Police investigating a crime are not subject to the DPA (e.g., a suspect cannot request to see evidence about them)
What is the intelligence services processing exemption from the DPA? | Personal data processed by intelligence services (e.g., MI5) is not covered by the DPA
What can companies face if they breach the Data Protection Act? | Extremely large fines
What security measures must companies implement under the DPA? | Physical or digital security measures to prevent data being accessed without consent
What must a company do when receiving a Subject Access Request? | Verify the identity of the subject and send a copy of their data securely
What does CMA stand for in computing legislation? | Computer Misuse Act
What does the Computer Misuse Act 1990 concern? | The malicious use of computers
Why was the Computer Misuse Act originally created? | To ensure that computer hacking was covered by law
How many primary offences does the Computer Misuse Act outline? | Three
State the first offence under the Computer Misuse Act | Unauthorised access to computer materials
State the second offence under the Computer Misuse Act | Unauthorised access with intent to commit further offences
State the third offence under the Computer Misuse Act | Unauthorised modification of computer material
Give an example of the first CMA offence (unauthorised access) | Finding a teacher's password and opening their files
Give an example of the second CMA offence (unauthorised access with intent) | Finding a teacher's password and opening their files with the intention of increasing your mark on their markbook
Give an example of the third CMA offence (unauthorised modification) | Finding a teacher's password, opening their files and increasing your mark on their markbook
Which CMA offences have worse consequences - the first or the second and third? | The second and third offences have generally worse consequences than the first
What punishment can each offence under the CMA result in? | Imprisonment
What security features can minimise the threat from unauthorised access? | Digital signatures or certificates
What can be used to prevent external people accessing a system and prevent DoS/DDoS attacks? | Firewalls
What does DoS stand for? | Denial of Service
What does DDoS stand for? | Distributed Denial of Service
What does the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 protect? | People's intellectual property (original work)
What types of work does the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act cover? | Everything from logos to music recordings, videos, audio, and computer programs
When does copyright automatically apply to a work? | When the work is original (automatic from creation)
How long does copyright last? | 25-70 years after the death of the creator, depending on the type of work
When was the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act extended to include computer programs? | 1992
How are computer programs classified under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act? | As a type of literary work
If someone believes their work has been copied, whose responsibility is it to take action? | The individual who believes their work has been copied (the copyright holder)
Name three primary breaches under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act | Copying an original work, issuing copies to the public, renting/lending copies to the public, performing/showing/playing the work in public, making an adaptation (any three)
Name three secondary breaches under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act | Importing a copy, possessing/dealing with a copy, providing means to make copies, permitting use of premises for copying, provision of props/equipment for performance of a copy (any three)
What is peer-to-peer streaming and why is it relevant to the Copyright Act? | It is sharing videos/audio between users, which prevents copyright owners from receiving income from their work
What does RIPA stand for? | Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act
What year was the Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act passed? | 2000
What does RIPA cover? | Investigation, surveillance and interception of communication by public bodies
What must be issued before intelligence services can legally wiretap online conversations under RIPA? | A warrant
What are ISPs required to do under RIPA regarding customer communications? | Give up information upon request from an authorised authority
What hardware requirement does RIPA place on ISPs and mobile phone companies? | Ensure their networks have sufficient hardware installed to facilitate surveillance
Why is RIPA controversial regarding its scope? | Its powers extend to small agencies like local councils
Give two criticisms of RIPA | Some people feel it is an invasion of privacy, or that it is often improperly used
What can public bodies demand from ISPs under RIPA? | Access to a customer's communications
What does RIPA allow regarding mass communications? | Mass surveillance of communications
What does RIPA demand regarding protected information? | Access to be granted to protected information
What does RIPA prevent regarding court proceedings? | The existence of interception activities being revealed in court
What does RIPA allow regarding internet activities? | Monitoring of an individual's internet activities
Which organisation has among the most freedom under RIPA? | GCHQ
What must businesses implement to comply with RIPA? | Hardware and software solutions that facilitate the storage of digital communications
