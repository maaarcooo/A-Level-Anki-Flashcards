What is Boolean logic used for in computer science? | Making logical decisions using operators that are either TRUE or FALSE
What two values can Boolean operators take? | TRUE or FALSE (often represented as 1 or 0)
What is the difference between Boolean logic and Boolean algebra? | Boolean logic refers to the principles of logic based on binary values; Boolean algebra is a mathematical system used to manipulate Boolean values
What is the order of precedence for evaluating Boolean expressions? | Brackets, then NOT, then AND, then OR
What are the four main Boolean operations? | Conjunction (AND), Disjunction (OR), Negation (NOT), and Exclusive Disjunction (XOR)
What is a truth table? | A table showing every possible permutation of inputs to a logic gate and the corresponding output
What is conjunction in Boolean logic? | The AND operation - returns TRUE only if both inputs are TRUE
What is the symbol for conjunction (AND)? | ∧ or . (dot)
What is the output of an AND gate when both inputs are TRUE? | TRUE (1)
What is the output of an AND gate when at least one input is FALSE? | FALSE (0)
Conjunction can be thought of as applying what arithmetic operation to binary inputs? | Multiplication
What is disjunction in Boolean logic? | The OR operation - returns TRUE if either input is TRUE
What is the symbol for disjunction (OR)? | ∨ or + (plus)
What is the output of an OR gate when both inputs are FALSE? | FALSE (0)
What is the output of an OR gate when at least one input is TRUE? | TRUE (1)
Disjunction can be thought of as applying what arithmetic operation to inputs? | Addition
What is negation in Boolean logic? | The NOT operation - inverts/reverses the truth value of the input
What is the symbol for negation (NOT)? | ¬ or a bar over the variable
How many inputs does a NOT gate have? | One input
What is NOT TRUE equal to? | FALSE
What is NOT FALSE equal to? | TRUE
What is exclusive disjunction (XOR)? | Returns TRUE only when exactly one input is TRUE, FALSE when both inputs are the same
What is the symbol for exclusive disjunction (XOR)? | ⊕ or ∨ with an underline
How does XOR differ from OR when both inputs are TRUE? | XOR outputs FALSE when both inputs are TRUE, while OR outputs TRUE
What does it mean for two Boolean expressions to be identical? | They have the same truth table
Why is it desirable to simplify Boolean expressions? | To use shorter versions that are easier to implement in circuits
What is a Karnaugh map used for? | Simplifying Boolean expressions
What is Gray code in the context of Karnaugh maps? | A coding system where adjacent columns and rows differ by only one bit
What constraint applies to Gray code in Karnaugh maps including wraparound? | Adjacent values can only differ by one bit, including from last column/row to first
How many variables can a Karnaugh map handle? | Two, three, or four variables
What shape must groups in a Karnaugh map be? | Rectangular
What sizes can groups in a Karnaugh map be? | Powers of 2: 1, 2, 4, or 8 cells
Can cells in a Karnaugh map belong to multiple groups? | Yes, overlapping is allowed and often beneficial
Can Karnaugh map groups wrap around edges? | Yes, the grid wraps around in all directions
When simplifying with a Karnaugh map, what should you aim for regarding rectangles? | Use as few rectangles as possible while making them as large as possible
What type of expression does a Karnaugh map produce when simplified? | Sum of products expression (terms OR'd together)
What are De Morgan's Laws used for? | Breaking a negation and changing the operator between two literals to simplify expressions
State De Morgan's first law | ¬(A ∧ B) ≡ ¬A ∨ ¬B (NOT of AND equals OR of NOTs)
State De Morgan's second law | ¬(A ∨ B) ≡ ¬A ∧ ¬B (NOT of OR equals AND of NOTs)
When applying De Morgan's Laws, what happens to the operator? | Conjunction is replaced by disjunction and vice versa
What is the distribution law in Boolean algebra? | Rules for how AND and OR interact: A ∧ (B ∨ C) ≡ (A ∧ B) ∨ (A ∧ C)
State the distribution law for conjunction over disjunction | A ∧ (B ∨ C) ≡ (A ∧ B) ∨ (A ∧ C)
State the distribution law for disjunction of conjunction | A ∨ (B ∧ C) ≡ (A ∨ B) ∧ (A ∨ C)
What is the associative law in Boolean algebra? | Rules for adding/removing brackets and reordering literals: (A ∧ B) ∧ C ≡ A ∧ (B ∧ C) ≡ A ∧ B ∧ C
State the associative law for AND | (A ∧ B) ∧ C ≡ A ∧ (B ∧ C) ≡ A ∧ B ∧ C
State the associative law for OR | (A ∨ B) ∨ C ≡ A ∨ (B ∨ C) ≡ A ∨ B ∨ C
What is the commutative law in Boolean algebra? | The order of literals around an operator does not matter
State the commutative law for AND | A ∧ B ≡ B ∧ A
State the commutative law for OR | A ∨ B ≡ B ∨ A
What is the double negation law? | Negating a literal twice returns the original value: ¬¬A ≡ A
What is NOT NOT A equal to? | A
What is X AND 0 equal to? | 0
What is X AND 1 equal to? | X
What is X AND X equal to? | X
What is NOT X AND X equal to? | 0
What is X OR 0 equal to? | X
What is X OR 1 equal to? | 1
What is X OR X equal to? | X
What is NOT X OR X equal to? | 1
What is a flip flop? | A type of logic circuit that can store the value of one bit
What is a D-type flip flop? | A flip flop that updates its output Q to the value of input D on the rising edge of a clock signal
What two inputs does a D-type flip flop have? | A data input (D) and a clock input (CLK)
What two outputs does a D-type flip flop have? | Q and NOT Q (Q̄)
When can the output of a D-type flip flop change? | Only at a rising edge of the clock (start of a clock tick)
What is a rising edge in a clock signal? | The transition from low to high
What is a falling edge in a clock signal? | The transition from high to low
What is a clock in digital circuits? | A regular pulse generated by the CPU used to coordinate computer components
What happens to Q in a D-type flip flop when D is high (1) at a rising edge? | Q goes high and NOT Q goes low
What happens to Q in a D-type flip flop when D is low (0) at a rising edge? | Q goes low and NOT Q goes high
What type of gates are D-type flip flops typically built from? | Four NAND gates
Why is a flip flop considered a basic unit of memory? | Because it can store one bit of data
What can sets of flip flops be combined to form? | Registers
What are D-type flip flops used for? | Shift registers, counters, memory units, edge-triggered devices, synchronous circuits, and data storage
Another name for D-type flip flop is? | Positive Edge Triggered flip flop
What is an adder circuit? | A logic circuit that adds together binary inputs and outputs the result in binary
What are the two types of adder circuits? | Half adder and full adder
How many inputs does a half adder have? | Two inputs (A and B)
How many outputs does a half adder have? | Two outputs (Sum and Carry)
What logic gates form a half adder? | One XOR gate and one AND gate
What does the XOR gate in a half adder produce? | The Sum output
What does the AND gate in a half adder produce? | The Carry output
In a half adder, when is the Carry output TRUE? | When both inputs A and B are TRUE (1)
In a half adder, when is the Sum output TRUE? | When exactly one of A or B is TRUE (but not both)
How does a full adder differ from a half adder? | A full adder has an additional input for carry in (Cin)
How many inputs does a full adder have? | Three inputs (A, B, and Cin)
How many outputs does a full adder have? | Two outputs (Sum and Cout)
What logic gates form a full adder? | Two XOR gates, two AND gates, and one OR gate
How many rows are in a full adder truth table? | Eight rows (compared to four for a half adder)
What does the Sum output of a full adder provide? | The XOR of all three inputs (A, B, and Cin)
When is the Carry output (Cout) of a full adder TRUE? | When at least two of the inputs A, B, and Cin are TRUE
What is a ripple adder? | Multiple full adders chained together where each Cout connects to the next Cin
How are full adders connected to form a ripple adder? | The carry out (Cout) of one adder connects to the carry in (Cin) of the next adder
How many full adders are needed to add two 4-bit binary numbers? | Four full adders
Why do full adders allow chaining while half adders don't easily? | Full adders have a carry input that can accept the carry output from a previous adder
What is a literal in Boolean algebra? | An input variable (like A or B)
What is a Boolean equation? | An equation that can equate to either True or False, but not both
What is NOT (TRUE AND FALSE) equal to? | TRUE
In a 2-variable Karnaugh map, how many cells are there? | Four cells
In a 3-variable Karnaugh map, how many cells are there? | Eight cells
In a 4-variable Karnaugh map, how many cells are there? | Sixteen cells
What does simplifying a Boolean expression using NAND and NOR gates allow? | Makes building microprocessors easier (e.g., for flash drives)
What is the absorption law A ∨ (A ∧ B) equal to? | A
What is the absorption law A ∧ (A ∨ B) equal to? | A
What is a bistable circuit? | A circuit with two stable states (like a flip flop)
Why might Boolean algebra simplification be preferred over Karnaugh maps? | Boolean algebra is more powerful and can simplify expressions that Karnaugh maps cannot
