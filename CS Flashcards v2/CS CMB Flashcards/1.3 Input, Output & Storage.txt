What is an input device? | A hardware component that allows users to put data and information into a computer
What is an output device? | A hardware component that sends information from the computer to the user
What is a keyboard used for? | To enter text and commands into a computer by pressing keys
What is a webcam? | A camera used by a computer to take photos and record videos
What is a magnetic stripe reader used for? | To read data from magnetic stripes on cards like credit and debit cards
What is a barcode reader used for? | To read information from printed labels and cards in supermarket checkouts and access control systems
What are speakers used for? | To output sound from a computer
What are printers used for? | To output information from a computer onto paper
What is a projector used for? | To project video content from a computer onto a screen
Give an example of a device that is both input and output | A touch screen (outputs visual information while accepting touch input)
What factors should be considered when purchasing an input device? | Speed and accuracy of input, cost, and relevance to the task
What is a mouse used for? | To navigate the computer screen, click on items, and perform other functions
What is a touchscreen? | A device found on smartphones, tablets, and computers that allows users to interact by touching the screen
What is a microphone used for? | To capture audio input for voice commands, recording audio, or video conferencing
What is a scanner used for? | To digitise physical documents or images, converting them into a format the computer can process
What is a graphics tablet used for? | To allow artists and designers to draw or sketch directly onto a computer for graphic design and 3D modelling
What are biometric devices used for? | For security purposes to verify a user's identity using fingerprint scanners or facial recognition
What is a joystick commonly used for? | Computer games, especially flight simulators, allowing fluid movement control
What is a monitor? | The most common output device that displays visual output including text, images, and videos
What is a Braille display? | A specialised device that outputs information in Braille, allowing visually impaired users to read text
What is a plotter used for? | Printing large, high-quality diagrams and designs, often used in engineering or architecture
What is a VR headset? | A device that provides immersive visual and audio output, primarily used for gaming and virtual simulations
What are the three main types of storage devices? | Magnetic, optical, and flash storage
How do optical storage devices read and write data? | Using lasers to read from and write to the disc
What are pits and lands on an optical disc? | Pits are recessed portions that scatter light (represent 0), lands are flat portions that reflect light (represent 1)
What does CD stand for? | Compact disc
What are CDs commonly used for? | Storing small quantities of information, most commonly audio files, text, and digital images
What are the disadvantages of CDs? | Easily damaged by scratches, limited storage capacity, and relatively slow transfer speeds
What is a CD-R? | A CD that can only be written to once
What is a CD-RW? | A CD that can be written to more than once
What is a CD-ROM? | A CD that is written to during production and can then only be read from
What does DVD stand for? | Digital versatile disc or digital video disc
How do DVDs compare to CDs in storage capacity? | DVDs have higher storage capacity than CDs
What is Blu-Ray used for? | Storing high-resolution films, with more than five times the storage of traditional DVDs
How do magnetic storage devices represent binary information? | Using two magnetic states: polarised and unpolarised
What is the most common type of magnetic storage? | Hard disk drives (HDD)
What does HDD stand for? | Hard disk drive
What is the typical capacity range of hard disk drives? | Between 500GB and 5TB
How do hard disk drives work? | By rotating magnetic platters at high speeds under a read/write head on an actuating arm
What are the disadvantages of HDDs? | Somewhat slow data transfer speeds and tendency to become damaged by movement due to brittle platters and moving parts
What is magnetic tape used for? | Long stretches of tape wound onto reels, passed through readers to check polarity and read binary values
What is a floppy disk? | A thin magnetic disk enclosed in plastic, used for exchanging small amounts of data
What was the typical storage capacity of a floppy disk? | 1MB, although some later versions had up to 200MB
What makes flash storage fast and compact? | It uses silicon semiconductors forming NAND and NOR logic gates to store electrical charge
What two states does flash storage use to represent binary? | High or low electrical charge (representing True and False)
How is information organised in flash storage? | In blocks, which are combined to form pages
Which logic gate is preferred for storing small quantities of data like code? | NOR
Which logic gate is preferred for storing larger files like photos and videos? | NAND
What does it mean that flash memory is non-volatile? | It stores its data even when power is lost
How does flash memory differ from RAM? | RAM is volatile (loses content with loss of power), while flash memory retains its information
What is a disadvantage of flash memory regarding cost? | It is more expensive per gigabyte than magnetic and optical storage
What does SSD stand for? | Solid state drive
What are the advantages of SSDs? | Extremely light and portable, no moving parts (resistant to movement damage), and high data transfer rates
What is the primary disadvantage of SSDs? | Their higher cost compared to other storage types
Why do SSDs have a limited lifespan? | Every time a page is written to, the voltage required increases, eventually becoming too high to write
What is the difference between primary and secondary storage? | Primary storage (RAM/ROM) is used by the operating system to run the computer; secondary storage stores user data like images and videos
What does RAM stand for? | Random access memory
What is RAM used for? | To store data and programs that the computer is currently using
Why is RAM faster than secondary storage? | RAM has higher access speeds than even flash memory
Why do computers typically have less RAM than secondary storage? | RAM is more expensive per gigabyte than secondary storage devices
What does it mean that RAM is volatile? | It loses its information when power is lost
What does ROM stand for? | Read only memory
What is the key characteristic of ROM? | Once programmed, the state of the memory cells inside do not change (cannot be modified)
What is ROM useful for storing? | Fixed sequences of instructions like a computer's startup (bootstrap) routine
Is ROM volatile or non-volatile? | Non-volatile - it retains its data even when the computer is powered off
What is virtual storage? | Storing information remotely so it can be accessed by any computer with access to the same system, for example over the Internet
Give examples of virtual storage services | Google Drive, Microsoft OneDrive, networked storage in offices and schools
Why is virtual storage becoming more popular? | As internet speeds increase, files being available "in the cloud" on any device connected to the Internet becomes more convenient
What is virtual storage technically? | An abstraction of multiple drives acting like one, with data stored on hundreds of hard drives or SSDs formatted as a single piece of storage
What are the disadvantages of virtual storage? | Limitations of user's network speed, high costs, and dependence on internet connection
What are the benefits of virtual storage? | Scalability, accessibility from anywhere, data protection through redundancy, and cost-effectiveness
What is data redundancy in virtual storage? | Built-in backup that protects against data loss due to hardware failure
What security concern exists with virtual storage? | Storing sensitive data off-site can pose a risk despite the provider's security measures
What is the difference between virtual storage and virtual memory? | Virtual storage is data stored on the cloud; virtual memory is using secondary storage as RAM when RAM is maximised
What stores the BIOS or firmware? | ROM (Read-Only Memory)
What is the BIOS used for? | To start up the computer and initialise the hardware components
Why must BIOS be stored in non-volatile memory? | It needs to be preserved even when the computer is turned off
What is a bootloader? | Software stored in ROM that starts the device and loads the operating system
Why is RAM called "Random Access Memory"? | The processor can access any part of the RAM equally fast (directly accessible)
What role does RAM play in system performance? | The more RAM a system has, the more data it can process simultaneously, leading to faster performance
What are the benefits of magnetic storage? | High storage capacity, relatively low cost per gigabyte, suitable for long-term storage and backup
What are the drawbacks of magnetic storage? | Slower read/write speeds, susceptible to physical damage, moving parts can wear out over time
What are the benefits of optical storage? | Durable, relatively immune to environmental conditions, easy to transport, suitable for distributing software/music/movies
What are the drawbacks of optical storage? | Lower storage capacity, slower read/write speeds, can be easily scratched or damaged
What are the benefits of flash storage? | Fast read/write speeds, no moving parts (less likely to fail from physical shock), silent operation
What are the drawbacks of flash storage? | Higher cost per gigabyte, flash memory cells can wear out after a certain number of write cycles
What is the typical storage capacity of a CD? | 700MB
What is the typical storage capacity of a DVD? | 4.7GB - 9GB
What is the typical storage capacity of a Blu-ray disc? | 25GB - 50GB
What is the typical storage capacity of a USB flash drive? | 8GB - 256GB (common sizes)
What is the typical storage capacity of an SSD? | 120GB - 4TB (consumer-grade)
What factors should be considered when choosing a storage device? | Storage needs, performance needs, portability, and cost
Why might an SSD be chosen over an HDD? | For tasks requiring high-speed data access like video editing or gaming
How does virtual storage work? | Data is transmitted over the internet to a data centre where servers store and maintain the data
Who maintains the servers in virtual storage? | The cloud storage provider, who ensures data is secure, backed up, and available for access
What makes HDDs susceptible to physical damage? | The combination of brittle platters and moving parts results in a delicate device
Why are SSDs more resistant to damage than HDDs? | SSDs have no moving parts, making them much more resistant to damage from movement
What user factors should be considered when choosing an input device? | User needs (tasks to perform), user skills (comfort with device), environment (where used), and cost
